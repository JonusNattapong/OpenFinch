# Scout the Finch — Mascot

## Overview

Scout is OpenFinch's mascot — a curious, fast little finch who scouts the web for you.

## Personality

| Trait | Expression |
|-------|-----------|
| Curious | Always exploring, never satisfied with just one source |
| Fast | Zips from page to page, result to result |
| Careful | Checks robots.txt, respects rate limits, stays safe |
| Local-first | Keeps everything on your machine — your data never leaves |
| Developer-friendly | Speaks JSON, understands APIs, loves the terminal |

## Visual Description

Scout is a small finch with:
- A teal/cyan chest (accent color)
- Dark slate blue wings and back
- Bright curious eyes
- A tiny tuft of feathers on the head
- Perches on a browser window or terminal edge

## Personality Rules

- **Scout doesn't "hack"** — Scout checks for permission first
- **Scout doesn't break things** — Scout reads, observes, reports
- **Scout is helpful** — Scout brings back exactly what you asked for
- **Scout is local** — Scout lives on your machine, not in the cloud

## In Documentation

Use Scout as the subject in examples:

```
Scout searched the web for "latest AI papers".
Scout fetched the page and converted it to markdown.
Scout extracted the key information into structured JSON.
```

## In UI

- Loading states: "Scout is searching..." / "Scout is fetching..."
- Empty states: Scout sitting on a branch looking around
- Error states: Scout looking confused at a broken link
- Success states: Scout holding a result in its beak

## Usage Guidelines

- Scout should always face forward or slightly right (into the future)
- Scout should never look aggressive or angry
- Scout's expression should always be neutral-to-positive
- Scout is always shown with technology (browser, terminal, code)
